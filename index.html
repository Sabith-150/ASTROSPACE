What is Light-Year ?

A light-year is a unit of distance used in astronomy to measure how far light travels in one year. Light moves at an incredible speed of approximately 300,000 kilometers per second (or 186,000 miles per second), and over the course of a year, it travels about 9.46 trillion kilometers (or 5.88 trillion miles). Despite its name, a light-year measures distance, not time, and it is primarily used to express the vast distances between celestial objects like stars and galaxies. For example, if a star is said to be 4 light-years away, it means the light we see from that star today actually left it 4 years ago, giving us a glimpse into the past. This makes the light-year an essential tool for understanding the enormous scale of the universe and the concept of observing distant objects as they existed in earlier times.
